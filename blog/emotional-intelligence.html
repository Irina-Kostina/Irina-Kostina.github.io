What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) is all about understanding emotions. Our own and other people’s. It means you can notice how you're feeling, manage those feelings, and respond to others in a thoughtful way. People with good emotional intelligence are usually good at staying calm under pressure, handling stress, listening to others, and getting along with different types of people. It’s not about being emotional. It’s about being smart with emotions. In everyday life, EQ helps to avoid saying things you’ll regret, solve problems without drama, and build better relationships with friends, family, or coworkers.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ (Emotional Intelligence) and IQ (Intelligence Quotient) are two different ways of being intelligent. IQ is about how well you think. It includes things like logic, problem-solving, memory, and learning. It’s often measured through tests and is linked to school and academic success. EQ is about how well you handle emotions—your own and other people’s. It’s more about how you communicate, deal with stress, show empathy, and work with others.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it helps to handle everyday life better. It’s what helps to stay calm during stress, talk things through instead of arguing, and understand how others feel. People with strong EQ usually build better relationships, work well in teams, and make more thoughtful decisions. In work and in life, emotions come up all the time. EQ gives you the tools to respond wisely instead of reacting quickly.

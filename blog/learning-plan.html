This blog post is a reflection on my core personal Learning Plan.

What is my long term goal or career pathway?

My long-term goal is to transition into a career in software development, find a great job and find the area in software development that suits me best. Possibly mobile app development or backend work. I want to become a skilled and reliable specialist in this field, ideally working in FinTech industry, because I have experience in finance and accounting. I’m aiming for a role that allows remote or flexible work so I can have a better balance in life.

Personally, I’d love to buy a house somewhere up north from Auckland, get a dog, work remotely, and have the freedom to travel more often.

A description of my strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Strengths:

— Self-disciplined and well-organised when working in a comfortable environment
— Curious and committed to lifelong learning
— Able to learn independently, seek out resources when stuck, and ask questions when needed
— Reflective and open to feedback


Limitations:

— Can feel overwhelmed when under pressure or when unclear about expectations or instructions
— Struggle with self-confidence when comparing myself to others
— I’m easily distracted in noisy or unstructured environments. I learn best in calm, quiet settings and may lose focus in loud surroundings I'm afraid and overwhelmed by thoughts of making a mistake by giving up my current career for something unknown, though I find it interesting

Non-technical skills I want to develop during my time at Dev Academy

While at Dev Academy, I would like to develop these crucial for developer human skills:
1. Communications.
I want to improve my ability to communicate with people who have different mindsets. It’s important to me to learn how to adapt to different communication styles while still staying myself. I also believe that effective, respectful communication is essential for any team to work well together.
2. Prioritising.
When there’s a lot to do, I can get overwhelmed and not know where to start. I want to learn how to focus on the most important tasks first and prioritise tasks.
3. Creativity.
I’d like to develop my creative thinking. Problem-solving in tech is often about finding new ways to approach old problems, and I want to train myself to think outside the box and not be afraid of trying something unusual.

My commitment to managing workload during The programme

I commit to managing my workload by staying organised, setting clear daily and weekly goals, and breaking down tasks into smaller, manageable steps. I’ll use tools like Toggle, to-do lists, or task boards to stay on track. I’ll also make sure to take regular breaks and maintain healthy boundaries to prevent burnout.

To work productively and safely with others, I’ll communicate openly and respectfully, listen actively, and be mindful of different mindsets and working styles. I’ll aim to contribute equally in group tasks and stay accountable for my part.

When I start to feel overwhelmed or notice stress building up, I’ll pause, take a short break, and reassess my priorities. I’ll ask for support if I need it and try to identify what exactly is causing the pressure. I’ll do my best to avoid burnout and keep learning effectively

My commitment to seeking help promptly and effectively

If I feel stuck or unsure about something, I commit to seeking help within 20–30 minutes rather than losing too much time trying to figure it out alone. I’ll first try to find answers by checking course resources, documentation, or my notes. If that doesn’t help, I’ll go and Google my questions and try to find answers online. And if that doesn’t help as well, I’ll reach out through the appropriate channels on Discord or ask a facilitator directly. I understand that seeking help is part of the learning process and not a weakness, and I’ll make an effort to be proactive and clear when looking for support.

What I expect from The Facilitation Team

I expect from Facilitation team to:

— Provide a safe, inclusive, and encouraging learning environment
— Communicate clearly about expectations and deadlines
— Offer timely, constructive feedback on my progress
— Be approachable and available for support when needed
— Show good human skills like active listening, empathy, and collaboration

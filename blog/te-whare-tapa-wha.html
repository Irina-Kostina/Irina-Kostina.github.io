What is Te Whare Tapa Whā

Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori model of health that compares wellbeing to the five walls of a wharenui (meeting house). Each wall represents a different aspect of wellbeing.

  • Taha Tinana – physical health
  • Hinengaro – mental and emotional health
  • Taha Whānau – family and social relationships
  • Taha Wairua – spiritual wellbeing
  • Whenua – connection to the land

Here is my personal wellbeing plan based on Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā model.

Taha Tinana

  • Taha Tinana – physical health
  • Hinengaro – mental and emotional health
  • Taha Whānau – family and social relationships
  • Taha Wairua – spiritual wellbeing
  • Whenua – connection to the land

Hinengaro

  • Journal for 5–10 minutes on how I feel
  • Take short breaks during study to avoid burnout
  • Talk to a friend or support person if I’m feeling overwhelmed
  • Take a long walk
  • Read a book

Taha Whānau

  • Have dinner with my partner every evening without screens
  • Call or video chat with my parents or friends

Taha Wairua

  • Start each day by saying one thing I’m grateful for
  • Spend quiet time alone each week to reflect
  • Read (or watch) something that inspires me

Whenua

  • Go for a walk in the park or along the beach every weekend or go for a hike
  • Grow herbs and plants at home
  • Learn about the local history and stories of the place I live in
